Of course functions as a sentence adverb in most cases: Of course I can help you with that. Some adverbs refer to a whole statement and not just a part of it. The expression was basically a concise way of conveying that something had followed or would follow the normal course of things; i.e., it was exactly how you'd expect it to be.
